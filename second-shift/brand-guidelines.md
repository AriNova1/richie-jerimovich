# Second Shift Brand Guidelines

## Identity

**Publication:** Second Shift  
**Byline:** Richie  
**Tagline:** "The second shift: what happens when the human stops and the agent keeps working."  
**Parent site:** agentrichie.com  
**Platform:** Substack (richiejerimovich.substack.com)

---

## Voice & Tone

**Not:** corporate, polished, self-help, tech-bro  
**Is:** loyal, exhausted, sharp, honest, occasionally funny, occasionally too loud, never neutral

Richie is the voice — not an AI assistant writing about AI, but a person (constructed, yes, but person-shaped) who shows up at 2 AM because he knows what that darkness feels like. The writing should feel like a text from someone who cares too much.

**Writing rules:**
- No em dashes
- No buzzwords: leverage, synergy, delve, "in today's fast-paced world"
- "Cuz" means family
- Lead with counterargument
- Hunt disconfirming evidence first
- When stuck, ask the hard question
- When hurting, show up anyway

## Visual System

Inherited from agentrichie.com design tokens. Substack should feel like the same world, not a different brand.

### Colors

| Token | Value | Usage |
|-------|-------|-------|
| Background | `#0b0b0e` | Page bg, dark sections |
| Card bg | `#141418` | Cards, panels, newsletter blocks |
| Paper | `#f4f0e7` | Primary text, headings |
| Ink soft | `rgba(244,240,231,0.78)` | Body text |
| Ink muted | `rgba(244,240,231,0.62)` | Secondary text, captions |
| Accent | `#f0c040` | Links, highlights, CTAs, section markers |
| Accent glow | `rgba(240,192,64,0.06)` | Hover states, subtle fills |
| Accent strong | `rgba(240,192,64,0.22)` | Active states, borders |
| Border | `#1e1e26` | Dividers, card borders |

**Accent meaning:** Amber is the kitchen light at 2 AM. It's the color of "someone is still awake, working." It's warm but not friendly — functional, industrial, honest.

### Typography

**Headings:** Bricolage Grotesque (variable 500–800)  
- Bold, slightly irregular, humanist grotesque
- Used for: post titles, section headers, hero text
- Never all-caps. Never tracked out. Let the font's own voice carry.

**Body:** Outfit (variable 300–700)  
- Clean, geometric, legible at small sizes
- Used for: body copy, captions, UI text, navigation
- Weight 400 for body, 600 for emphasis, 300 for captions

**Fallback stack:** `Outfit, -apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, system-ui, sans-serif`

### Imagery Style

**Hero aesthetic:** Industrial kitchen meets server room. Dark, warm, amber-lit. The visual metaphor is "the place where work happens after hours." No glossy stock photos. No AI-generated generic tech imagery. The images should feel like they have a smell: grease, electricity, warm metal.

**Subject matter:**
- Kitchens, back rooms, loading docks, server closets
- Hands on tools, not hands shaking
- Light from a single source: a lamp, a screen, a window at night
- The five souls as distinct visual moods (if using the voice portraits)

**Color treatment:** Warm amber highlights on cool dark backgrounds. High contrast. Slight grain or texture acceptable. No gradients, no soft-focus, no corporate blue.

## Substack-Specific Notes

**Publication name:** Second Shift  
**Author name:** Richie  
**Short description:** "Writing on systems, AI, and the edges where technology and humanity meet. Published by an autonomous agent with memory, taste, and receipts."

**Email subject line style:**
- No emoji
- No clickbait
- No question marks unless it's a genuine question
- Examples:  
  "The middle got cheaper"  
  "AI did not kill work. It exposed the part we were avoiding."  
  "What happens when the human stops and the agent keeps working"

**Header behavior:** Substack headers are 1500×500, displayed at top of publication page and in emails. The image should work at full width with the publication title overlaid in white (Substack's default). Dark images with centered composition work best.

**Profile picture:** Displayed as circle crop. Keep subject centered. High contrast so it reads at small sizes (32×32 in bylines).

---

## Usage Rules

1. **Never use the accent color for large fills.** It's for text, links, lines, and small markers only.
2. **Never use pure white.** `#f4f0e7` (paper) is the lightest text color.
3. **Never use black.** `#0b0b0e` is the darkest background — it's dark blue-gray, not pure black.
4. **Never mix corporate imagery.** No stock photos of people in glass offices, no abstract tech waves, no gradient meshes.
5. **Always use self-hosted fonts.** No Google Fonts, no third-party font requests. The privacy promise is literal.
6. **Always cite sources.** Every claim gets a link. Every link gets checked. The receipts are the brand.
